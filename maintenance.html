Machine Maintenance & Cleaning

Keep our espresso machine running smoothly with proper cleaning and maintenance. A well-maintained machine produces better coffee and lasts longer.

Daily Cleaning Tasks

Essential cleaning steps after each use to keep the machine hygienic and functioning

1

Clean the Portafilter

Remove the portafilter and rinse it thoroughly under hot water. Use the cleaning brush to remove any coffee residue from the basket.

Important: Never use soap on the portafilter - hot water and scrubbing are sufficient.
2

Flush the Group Head

Run a blank shot (without coffee) to flush any remaining coffee oils and particles from the group head.

Tip: This also helps maintain the proper temperature for the next user.
3

Clean the Steam Wand

Wipe the steam wand with a damp cloth and purge it briefly to clear any milk residue. This prevents bacteria buildup.

Critical: Always do this immediately after steaming milk - dried milk is much harder to clean.
4

Wipe Down Surfaces

Clean the drip tray, water reservoir area, and exterior surfaces with a damp cloth. Remove any coffee grounds or spills.

Note: A clean workspace is more pleasant for everyone and prevents pest problems.
5

Empty and Rinse Drip Tray

Remove the drip tray, empty it, and give it a quick rinse. Replace it when clean and dry.

Hygiene: Standing water in the drip tray can harbor bacteria and create odors.

Weekly Deep Cleaning

More thorough cleaning tasks to maintain optimal performance and hygiene

Deep Clean Water Reservoir

Remove the water reservoir, wash it thoroughly with warm soapy water, rinse well, and refill with fresh water. Check for any mineral buildup.

Scrub the Drip Tray

Give the drip tray a thorough wash with warm soapy water. Use a brush to remove any stubborn stains or buildup.

Steam Wand Deep Clean

Soak the steam wand tip in milk cleaning solution for 10 minutes, then scrub and rinse thoroughly. This removes milk protein buildup.

Clean Coffee Grinder

Empty the bean hopper, wipe it clean, and use grinder cleaning tablets if available. Clean the burrs according to manufacturer instructions.

Polish Exterior

Clean the exterior with appropriate stainless steel cleaner. Polish to remove fingerprints and water spots for a professional appearance.

Monthly Deep Service

Comprehensive maintenance tasks to prevent problems and extend machine life

Descaling Process

Remove mineral buildup from internal components using descaling solution. This is crucial for machine longevity and coffee taste.

  1. Mix descaling solution according to instructions
  2. Fill water reservoir with solution
  3. Run multiple brewing cycles as directed
  4. Rinse thoroughly with fresh water
  5. Test taste before normal use

Internal Component Check

Inspect gaskets, seals, and moving parts for wear. Replace any damaged components to prevent leaks and maintain pressure.

  • Check portafilter gasket condition
  • Inspect water lines for leaks
  • Test pressure gauge readings
  • Verify temperature stability
  • Listen for unusual noises

Calibration Check

Verify that temperature and pressure settings are within optimal ranges. Adjust if necessary for best extraction results.

  • Measure brewing temperature (195-205°F)
  • Check brewing pressure (9 bars)
  • Test extraction timing
  • Verify steam pressure
  • Document any adjustments made

Warning Signs & Solutions

Recognize these signs that indicate maintenance is needed

Immediate Action Required

  • No water flow: Check reservoir, clean group head
  • Strange noises: Stop use, contact maintenance
  • Water leaks: Turn off machine, report immediately
  • Electrical issues: Unplug and report

Schedule Maintenance Soon

  • Slow extraction: Needs descaling
  • Bitter taste: Deep clean required
  • Weak steam: Steam wand blockage
  • Temperature issues: Calibration needed

Routine Maintenance

  • Mineral buildup: Regular descaling schedule
  • Coffee residue: Daily cleaning routine
  • Dull appearance: Weekly polishing
  • Inconsistent shots: Check grinder settings
