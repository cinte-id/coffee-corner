Milk Steaming Basics

Essential techniques for creating perfect milk foam and texture

Start Cold

Use cold milk straight from the refrigerator. Cold milk steams better and creates more foam.

Fill Pitcher

Fill the milk pitcher about 1/3 full. This gives room for the milk to expand as it foams.

Create Foam

Keep the steam wand tip just below the surface for the first few seconds to create foam.

Heat Through

Plunge the wand deeper to heat the milk to 150-160°F. The pitcher should be too hot to hold.
